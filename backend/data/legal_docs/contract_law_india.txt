Section 1: Short Title and Commencement
This Act may be called the Indian Contract Act, 1872. It extends to the whole of India.

Section 2: Definitions
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
    a. "Contract" means an agreement enforceable by law.
    b. "Agreement" means a promise or a set of promises forming the consideration for each other.

Section 3: Proposal and Acceptance
A proposal is made when one person signifies to another his willingness to do or abstain from doing something, with a view to obtaining the assent of the other to such act or abstinence.

Section 4: Consideration for Contracts
For an agreement to become a contract, there must be lawful consideration, which means something of value that is exchanged between the parties.

Section 5: Capacity of Parties
A contract is only valid if the parties entering into the contract are competent to contract. They must be of legal age, of sound mind, and not disqualified by law.
